Agent reading

Plain language summary · cited

Aggregation is the dominant historical driver of shelf-life determination for IgG1 mAbs in histidine formulations JUS-A ¶4JUS-C ¶3 — surfaced as shelf-life-limiting in 6 of 8 prior justifications reviewed. For mAb-Y, the current HMW trajectory at 5°C is consistent with prior IgG1 mAbs in the same buffer family, projecting 3.9% at 36 months against a 5.0% specification — comparable to mAb-X at the equivalent stage JUS-A ¶5.

A secondary risk has emerged on the acidic peak (deamidation). The observed rate at 5°C (+0.17 %/mo) is faster than the equivalent rate for mAb-X (+0.11 %/mo) and projects 20.4% at 36 months against a 19% upper limit. The differential is consistent with pH-dependent deamidation kinetics — mAb-Y is at pH 5.2, mAb-X at pH 5.5 — a known effect for IgG1 CDR asparagine residues OOT-Y ¶H3TAC-INT ¶Q6. This trajectory is the subject of an active OOT investigation OOT-2025-014.

Potency loss over accelerated conditions matches the historical pattern for cell-based potency in this class JUS-A ¶6 and does not appear as a shelf-life-limiting attribute at the proposed 36-month claim.
