Proposed shelf-life claim
36 months at 2–8°C
Under EMA MAA · EMEA/H/C/XXXXXX · CHMP Day 120 response window
Primary argument

Linear regression on 18 months of long-term stability data at 5°C across three GMP batches (BY-2024-001, BY-2024-002, BY-2023-005) projects HMW aggregation to 3.9% at 36 months against a shelf-life specification of NMT 5.0%. The projection is supported by 95% confidence intervals and consistent kinetics across the three batches (CV < 5%). JUS-A ¶5MEMO-X-COMP

Supporting arguments

• Arrhenius extrapolation from accelerated data at 25°C and 40°C provides orthogonal confirmation of the 36-month claim. Linear kinetics at all three temperatures verified prior to invoking Arrhenius as supporting evidence. JUS-A ¶6

• Comparability demonstrated across three registration batches: coefficient of variation below 5% at all timepoints for all shelf-life-critical attributes. MEMO-X-COMP ¶5

• Precedent: mAb-X (anti-CD20, IgG1, histidine pH 5.5) was accepted at initial CHMP review for 24 months using this same argument pattern. JUS-A — accepted CHMP 2022-11 with no query on shelf-life claim.

Alternatives considered
Arrhenius as primary argument — rejected
Not proposed. Precedent from mAb-Z (JUS-B original, 2023) shows FDA rejects Arrhenius as primary when kinetics are not demonstrably linear across the assessed temperature range. Present kinetics for mAb-Y are near-linear at 40°C, but the risk of a query outweighs the argument's strength. Retained as supporting. JUS-B-orig ¶3LEARN:JUS-B-01
Tighten specification to gain longer claim — deferred
Considered (per MIN-X ¶3 precedent from mAb-X review board). Tightening HMW spec from NMT 5.0% to NMT 4.5% would require re-justification of clinical batches. Not advisable at this stage. Reserved for post-approval discussion.
Disclosed risk factors
Acidic peak trending toward upper limit
Deamidation trajectory at pH 5.2 projects the acidic peak reaching 20.4% at 36 months against a spec of 11–19%. This risk was identified in OOT-2025-014 and is disclosed proactively in the Day 120 response. Contingency: 30-month claim reserved if the 30-month timepoint confirms the projection.
Post-approval commitments

• Submit 24-month real-time data across all three registration batches at completion (planned Q2 2027) as part of variation procedure.

• Submit 30-month timepoint data as soon as available (planned Q4 2026) to confirm acidic peak trajectory ahead of shelf-life boundary.
