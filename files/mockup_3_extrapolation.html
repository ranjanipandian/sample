Accepted

"Long-term data at 5°C demonstrates a stable HMW profile with mean increase of 0.8% per 12 months, projecting a mean HMW at 24 months of 3.2%, well within the shelf-life specification of NMT 4.0%."

Accepted

"Arrhenius-based extrapolation using accelerated data at 25°C independently supports the same 24-month shelf life, providing orthogonal confirmation."
Two independent methods converging is what made this accepted. Arrhenius as sole primary argument has been rejected — see right panel.

Extrapolation arguments — rejected

1 REJECTION IN GRAPH
Arrhenius extrapolation as primary argument
Rejected by FDA
mAb-Z · JUS-B-orig ¶3 · FDA · 2023-04
"The Arrhenius model requires linear kinetics across the temperature range assessed. The applicant's own data at 40°C shows non-Arrhenius behaviour beyond 3 months, suggesting a secondary degradation pathway becomes dominant. The Arrhenius extrapolation to 36 months is therefore not supported by the data submitted."
Learning applicable to mAb-Y
Before proposing Arrhenius as a primary argument, verify linear kinetics across the full temperature range (5/25/40°C). For mAb-Y, HMW kinetics at 40°C are near-linear through 6 months — Arrhenius as supporting is defensible; Arrhenius as primary is not.
Captured to graph LEARN:JUS-B-01 · applicable to any future large-molecule extrapolation
Cross-graph note
Two prior IgG1 mAbs (mAb-X, mAb-Y candidate) have used the acceptance-friendly pattern (linear as primary + Arrhenius as supporting). No prior IgG1 case in the graph has succeeded with Arrhenius alone.
Recommended approach for mAb-Y
Linear regression on real-time data as primary · Arrhenius from accelerated as supporting
